Voice Note #12 - SSL, Email Infrastructure & Caching
Date: 2026-02-02

---

You should have SSL transparency reports, so you know whenever a new SSL is issued for your domains.
You should have proper email infrastructure, meaning proper MX records.
SPF for the sender policy framework, DKIM for email encryption,
DMARK as well to know if your emails are always passing these.
You should not play around with this.
Every once in a while, you should also do tests on the placement of your emails with new fresh Gmail accounts.
Where are the emails placed?
You should have something like a spam assassin to measure your email performance.
You should be able to know every email that goes out of your domain.
You need full control over your transactional emails and logs about them.
You should be able to see the bounces there and the open rates and the click rates.
For your email, your marketing emails as well, you should be able to have a place where you can log them
and actually see who's it sent to.
You don't need to give them for more than two weeks or a month at the latest.
But being able to actively look at them and see their open rates, their click rates, that's critical.
You should have headers to make sure that your website is always loaded using HPS.
If you can have proper content hash on your script tags and CSS.
Your JavaScript CSS images should be fully cached by Cloudflare.
You should have cache busters on your resources if they change.
